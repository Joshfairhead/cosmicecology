3.8.3 Eternity as the Condition of Potentiality

The interchangeability between potential and actual existence—eternity functioning as the storehouse of potentialities—tree as illustrative example—characteristics of eternity as multivalent, synchronous, reversible, and imperishable—inversion of thermodynamic principles—virtue reconceptualized in terms of negative entropy—apokrisis as interval within eternity explained through paper sheet analogy—three distinct forms of consciousness—various defining features of eternity.
